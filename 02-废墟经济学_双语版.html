废墟经济学：注意力资本对实体空间的掠夺

The Economics of Ruins: How the Attention Economy Dispossesses Physical Space
Dai Shiqi  ·  School of Architecture, Tsinghua University
M.Arch in Architecture and Urban Design  ·  Urban Spatial Intelligence
Submitted: April 2025  ·  Revised: April 2025
摘要 Abstract
本文考察注意力经济（attention economy）对实体城市空间的系统性侵蚀。以天津五大道的衰败与谷歌圣何塞 Downtown West 的废墟为双重镜像，文章援引 Harvey（2003）的"剥夺性积累"理论与 Zuboff（2019）的监控资本主义框架，论证数字资本的兴起并非空间的解放，而是一种新形式的空间掠夺：实体空间被抽空经济活力后沦为废墟，文明记忆在流量逻辑中被商品化乃至遗弃。本文拒绝将这一过程视为技术进步的必然代价，主张从阶级视角重新审视"谁拥有空间、谁失去空间"这一根本问题。
Attention Economy Platform Urbanism Dispossession Heritage Digital Capital Class Urban Decay
Abstract
This article examines the systematic erosion of physical urban space by the attention economy. Using the decay of Tianjin's Five Avenues and the ruins of Google's Downtown West in San José as twin mirrors, it draws on Harvey's (2003) theory of accumulation by dispossession and Zuboff's (2019) framework of surveillance capitalism to argue that the rise of digital capital does not liberate space but rather dispossesses it in a new form: physical spaces are hollowed of economic vitality and left as ruins, while civilizational memory is commodified—then abandoned—by the logic of platform traffic. This article refuses to read this process as an inevitable cost of technological progress and insists on a class analysis of the fundamental question: who owns space, and who loses it?
I.
一个废墟，两种叙事
One Ruin, Two Narratives
2021年，谷歌以超过1.1亿美元从圣何塞市购入80英亩土地，承诺建造一座"城市之心而非郊区办公园区"的 Downtown West——拥有7.3百万平方英尺的办公空间、4,000套住宅、15英亩滨水公园，以及对周边社区190亿美元的经济拉动。两年后，当科技股下跌、广告收入萎缩、混合办公重塑劳动力需求，谷歌解散了开发团队，无限期暂停建设。留下的，是一片已被清除历史建筑的空地，铁丝网围栏，以及搬迁后再也无法恢复元气的本地餐厅。
In 2021, Google purchased 80 acres of land from San José for over $110 million, promising to build a Downtown West described as "the center of a city, not a suburban office park"—7.3 million square feet of offices, 4,000 housing units, 15 acres of riverfront parks, and a projected $19 billion in economic impact for the surrounding community. Two years later, as tech stocks fell, advertising revenues contracted, and hybrid work reshaped labor demand, Google disbanded its development team and indefinitely suspended construction. What remained was a cleared swath of land where historic buildings once stood: barbed-wire fencing, and local restaurants relocated to unfamiliar blocks from which they never recovered.
与此同时，在地球另一端的天津，五大道——那片曾聚集梁启超、张学良、袁世凯等历史人物的英租界遗存，拥有逾2,000栋风格各异的近代建筑——正以一种更缓慢、更难以名状的方式衰败。它被纳入4A景区，被重新包装为"打卡地"，被社交媒体的凝视所消费，原住民迁出，商业资本涌入，历史的肌理在网红滤镜下变得既真实又虚假。经济衰退叠加注意力经济的逻辑，使这片空间陷入一种奇特的困境：它被看见，却未被保护；它被消费，却未被珍视（Xiang et al., 2023）。
Meanwhile, on the other side of the planet, Tianjin's Five Avenues—the former British Concession where historical figures including Liang Qichao, Zhang Xueliang, and Yuan Shikai once resided, comprising more than 2,000 villas in diverse architectural styles—was decaying in a slower, less legible manner. Repackaged as a 4A-level tourist destination, consumed by the gaze of social media, its original residents displaced as commercial capital moved in, the district's historical fabric became simultaneously real and simulated beneath the filters of influencer photography. Economic stagnation compounded by the logic of the attention economy has trapped this space in a peculiar predicament: it is seen, but not protected; consumed, but not cherished (Xiang et al., 2023).
废墟，并非历史偶然的残余——它是资本撤退后留下的签名。谁创造了废墟，谁就拥有了对空间的定义权。
A ruin is not the accidental residue of history—it is the signature capital leaves behind upon withdrawal. Whoever creates the ruin owns the power to define what space means.
这两个案例，一个在美国科技资本的核心地带，一个在中国工业城市的历史街区，表面上毫无关联。但将它们并置，却揭示出同一套逻辑的两张面孔：注意力经济对实体空间的掠夺——有时以投资之名，有时以凝视之名，有时以遗忘之名，但结果总是相似的：空间被抽空，人被置换，历史被商品化或废弃。
These two cases—one at the center of American tech capital, the other in a historic district of a Chinese industrial city—appear superficially unrelated. Yet placing them side by side reveals two faces of the same logic: the dispossession of physical space by the attention economy. Sometimes in the name of investment, sometimes in the name of spectacle, sometimes through neglect—but the outcome is consistently similar: space is hollowed out, people are displaced, and history is commodified or abandoned.

II.
剥夺的新形式：从土地到行为，再到空间的空洞化
A New Form of Dispossession: From Land to Behaviour to the Hollowing of Space
马克思主义地理学家 David Harvey（2003）将"剥夺性积累"（accumulation by dispossession）定义为资本主义持续通过掠夺公共财富、私有化公共资源、金融化土地与住房来维系自身扩张的机制——它并非资本主义的"原始"阶段，而是其永恒的运行逻辑。在 Harvey 的框架中，城市空间是资本过度积累的缓冲器与战场：当资本找不到足够的生产性投资出口，它便转向城市土地，以建设热潮吸纳过剩，而当热潮退去，留下的往往是债务、空置与被驱逐的人。圣何塞的废地，在这个框架下，并不令人意外。
Marxist geographer David Harvey (2003) defines "accumulation by dispossession" as capitalism's perpetual mechanism of sustaining expansion through the seizure of public wealth, privatization of public resources, and financialization of land and housing—not a "primitive" phase of capitalism but its enduring operational logic. In Harvey's framework, urban space serves as both buffer and battleground for overaccumulated capital: when capital cannot find sufficient productive investment outlets, it turns toward urban land, absorbing surpluses through construction booms, and when the boom collapses, it leaves behind debt, vacancy, and displaced populations. The wasteland of San José is, within this framework, entirely predictable.
然而 Harvey 的框架尚未完全捕捉到注意力经济时代的新维度。Shoshana Zuboff（2019）在其监控资本主义理论中揭示了一种更深层的掠夺：数字平台将人类的行为、注意力与情感体验视为原材料，提取、量化，转化为"行为期货"出售给广告商。这一过程的关键在于，它需要不断开拓新的"行为领地"——包括城市空间本身。当谷歌以一个"混合社区"的愿景承诺改造圣何塞市中心，它同时也在将城市居民的日常行为纳入自身的数据生产体系；当天津五大道被改造为可被 Instagram 算法识别的打卡景观，它的历史信息就在被抽取的同时被扁平化（Törnberg, 2025）。
Yet Harvey's framework does not fully capture the new dimensions introduced by the attention economy. Shoshana Zuboff (2019) reveals a deeper layer of dispossession in her theory of surveillance capitalism: digital platforms treat human behaviour, attention, and emotional experience as raw material—extracted, quantified, and sold to advertisers as "behavioural futures." The critical dynamic here is that this process requires the continuous colonisation of new "behavioural territories," including urban space itself. When Google promised to transform downtown San José into a "mixed-use village," it was simultaneously incorporating the daily behaviour of city residents into its data-production apparatus; when Tianjin's Five Avenues were remoulded into a landscape legible to Instagram's algorithms, their historical information was simultaneously extracted and flattened (Törnberg, 2025).
Case · 案例
Törnberg（2025）对圣保罗绅士化街区投资者的民族志研究表明，社交媒体的逻辑已深入城市建成环境的生产机制：投资者根据其对"平台上什么内容有效"的判断来决定在哪栋建筑中注资，每隔几个月更换室内装饰以满足社交媒体对新奇感的永恒渴求。Zukin（1989）所描述的绅士化美学——通过叙述"可欲性"来实现价值的自我实现——在注意力经济中被平台算法所接管与加速。原住民社区在这一过程中系统性地从图像中消失，正如他们从街区中消失一样。
Törnberg's (2025) ethnographic study of investors in São Paulo's gentrifying Centro district reveals that the logic of social media has penetrated deep into the mechanisms of urban built-environment production: investors determine in which buildings to invest based on their understanding of "what works" on platforms, and renovate interiors every few months to satisfy social media's perpetual appetite for novelty. The gentrification aesthetic Zukin (1989) described—narrativising "desirability" to instantiate rising value—is, in the attention economy, hijacked and accelerated by platform algorithms. In this process, longstanding residents systematically disappear from images just as they disappear from neighbourhoods.
Banks（2023）将这一逻辑命名为"城市真实性政治"（city authentic）：在注意力经济中，城市的经济上升依赖于其在数字媒体上的"真实感"叙事——铁锈带工业废墟、殖民时期的砖石街道、历史名人故居——恰恰因其"衰败的美感"而获得流量价值，从而吸引外部资本进入，驱逐原有居民，最终摧毁产生这种"真实感"的社会条件本身。这是一个自我吞噬的悖论：注意力经济消费废墟，然后制造更多废墟。
Banks (2023) names this logic the politics of the "city authentic": in the attention economy, a city's economic ascent depends on its narrative of "authenticity" in digital media—Rust Belt industrial ruins, colonial brick streets, the former residences of historical figures—which acquire traffic value precisely through their "aesthetic of decay," thereby attracting external capital, displacing existing residents, and ultimately destroying the social conditions that produced the "authenticity" in the first place. This is a self-devouring paradox: the attention economy consumes ruins, then manufactures more of them.

III.
赛博：一个阶级概念
Cyber: A Class Concept
"赛博"（cyber）在大众叙事中往往被呈现为一个中性的技术描述词——数字的、连接的、虚拟的。但这种中性化本身，正是意识形态运作的方式。赛博空间并非平等开放的公共领域：它是由服务器基础设施、算法权力、数据所有权和平台规则所结构的阶级空间。拥有这些基础设施的人，拥有的不只是技术资产，而是对现实的定义权——包括对城市空间价值的定义权。
"Cyber" in popular discourse is typically presented as a neutral technical descriptor—digital, connected, virtual. But this neutralisation is itself ideology at work. Cyberspace is not an equally open public commons: it is a class space structured by server infrastructure, algorithmic power, data ownership, and platform governance. Those who own this infrastructure own not merely technical assets but the power to define reality itself—including the power to define the value of urban space.
Harvey（2012）在《叛逆城市》中写道，城市化从来不是中性的技术过程，而是阶级权力的地理表达：谁有权塑造城市，谁就有权决定谁被包容、谁被驱逐。注意力经济将这一逻辑延伸至数字层面：谷歌承诺圣何塞的"城市愿景"，本质上是一份单方面的城市改造协议，其条款由私人资本起草，其收益由股东持有，其风险由城市居民承担——而当资本撤退时，居民所能收回的，只有清空的土地和对已消逝事物的记忆。
Harvey (2012) argues in Rebel Cities that urbanisation has never been a neutral technical process but the geographical expression of class power: those who have the right to shape the city have the right to determine who is included and who is expelled. The attention economy extends this logic to the digital layer: Google's "urban vision" for San José was fundamentally a unilateral urban transformation agreement whose terms were drafted by private capital, whose returns accrued to shareholders, and whose risks were borne by city residents—and when capital withdrew, what residents could reclaim was only cleared land and memories of what had been lost.
当价值从砖石迁移到服务器，穷人失去的不只是房子——他们失去的是被城市承认存在的资格。
When value migrates from brick and mortar to servers, what the poor lose is not only housing—they lose the very right to be acknowledged as existing within the city.
天津五大道的困境揭示了注意力经济阶级逻辑的另一个维度：当数字平台成为城市体验的中介，"谁的叙事被看见"这一问题就变成了"谁的存在被承认"的问题。Törnberg 和 Uitermark（2022）的研究表明，社交媒体系统性地放大了消费阶层的城市体验，而令长期居民的生活——其复杂性、其与空间的情感联结、其非奇观性的日常——从数字图像中消失。在这个意义上，注意力经济不仅是空间资源的再分配机制，更是一种符号暴力：它决定什么空间"值得"被记录，进而决定什么文明"值得"被保存。
The predicament of Tianjin's Five Avenues reveals another dimension of the attention economy's class logic: when digital platforms mediate urban experience, the question of "whose narrative is seen" becomes the question of "whose existence is acknowledged." Research by Törnberg and Uitermark (2022) demonstrates that social media systematically amplifies the urban experiences of the consuming class while rendering the lives of long-term residents—their complexity, their emotional attachment to space, their non-spectacular everydayness—invisible in the digital image. In this sense, the attention economy is not only a mechanism for redistributing spatial resources but a form of symbolic violence: it determines which spaces are "worthy" of documentation, and thereby which civilisations are "worthy" of preservation.

IV.
文明的珍宝与流量的逻辑：不可调和的张力
The Treasures of Civilisation and the Logic of Traffic: An Irreconcilable Tension
历史城区的保护危机，并非简单的"资金不足"问题，而是价值体系的根本冲突。Halbwachs（1992）的集体记忆理论告诉我们，场所不只是记忆的容器，它本身参与记忆的建构：当一个街区被清空、被商品化、被改造为打卡景点，居民在其中积累的集体记忆便随之瓦解——不是因为记忆被遗忘，而是因为承载记忆的空间关系被切断了。这种切断是不可逆的。Patty's Inn 在圣何塞已经消失，五大道的原住民也已散居他处；即便谷歌明天重启建设，即便五大道的砖石明天被修缮一新，失去的社会肌理也无法复原。
The preservation crisis facing historic urban districts is not simply a matter of insufficient funding but a fundamental conflict between value systems. Halbwachs's (1992) theory of collective memory reveals that place is not merely a container for memory but an active participant in its construction: when a neighbourhood is cleared, commodified, and transformed into a photo-opportunity destination, the collective memory accumulated by its residents dissolves—not because memory is forgotten, but because the spatial relationships that carry memory are severed. This severance is irreversible. Patty's Inn in San José has already disappeared; the original residents of the Five Avenues have already dispersed. Even if Google restarts construction tomorrow, even if the Five Avenues' stonework is restored to pristine condition tomorrow, the lost social fabric cannot be reconstituted.
注意力经济对历史遗产的处理方式，揭示了它与文明保护之间根本性的不相容：流量逻辑需要新鲜感与奇观性，而文明的珍贵恰恰在于其厚重、缓慢、拒绝被快速消费的品质。Nora（1989）所说的"记忆之场"（lieux de mémoire）——那些在历史断裂中守护集体身份的场所——在注意力经济中要么被奇观化（变成打卡点）、要么被遗弃（因为无法产生流量而失去投资）。两种命运都是毁灭，只是速度与形式不同。
The attention economy's treatment of historical heritage reveals a fundamental incompatibility with civilisational preservation: the logic of traffic demands novelty and spectacle, while the precious quality of civilisation lies precisely in its weight, its slowness, its refusal to be rapidly consumed. The "lieux de mémoire"—the sites of memory that Nora (1989) identified as guardians of collective identity across historical rupture—are, in the attention economy, either spectacularised (transformed into photo destinations) or abandoned (deprived of investment because they cannot generate traffic). Both fates are forms of destruction; they differ only in speed and form.
更深的问题在于：数字平台所生产的"空间表征"（representation of space）——借用 Lefebvre（1991）的概念——正在取代人们对实体空间的感知与记忆。当一个人对五大道的认知主要来自小红书的图片推送，而非亲身漫步于梧桐树下的身体体验，空间的意义便从具身性的感知转变为符号性的消费。这不只是一个感知问题，而是政治问题：被符号化的空间更容易被资本重新定义和重新定价，因为它已经脱离了与使用者之间的情感契约。
The deeper problem is this: the "representations of space"—to use Lefebvre's (1991) concept—produced by digital platforms are increasingly displacing people's embodied perception of and memory for physical space. When a person's understanding of the Five Avenues derives primarily from algorithmically curated images on social media rather than the bodily experience of strolling beneath plane trees, the meaning of space shifts from embodied perception to symbolic consumption. This is not merely a problem of perception but of politics: symbolised space is more easily redefined and repriced by capital, precisely because it has been severed from the emotional contract between space and its users.

V.
恐惧与好奇之间：一个不情愿的目击者的立场
Between Fear and Curiosity: The Position of an Unwilling Witness
我不是一个乐观主义者。我亲历过天津经济停滞的年代，见过五大道在商业化浪潮中如何失去它的灵魂——不是在某一个戏剧性的时刻，而是在无数个细节的流失中：店面的置换，老住户的迁出，建筑立面的"修缮"，以及那种说不清楚的、属于一个街区的生活气息的消散。我对赛博化趋势感到恐惧，不是因为我不理解数字技术的力量，而恰恰是因为我理解它——理解它在重新分配空间价值上的效率，以及这种效率对于那些没有数字资产的人意味着什么。
I am not an optimist. I witnessed Tianjin's years of economic stagnation and watched the Five Avenues lose its soul under the tide of commercialisation—not in any single dramatic moment but through the accumulation of countless small losses: the turnover of storefronts, the departure of longtime residents, the "restoration" of building facades, and the dissolution of that ineffable quality of life that belongs to a neighbourhood. My fear of the cyber-turn is not born of failure to understand the power of digital technology—it comes precisely from understanding it: understanding its efficiency in redistributing spatial value, and what that efficiency means for those who hold no digital assets.
同时，我也不愿陷入一种无能为力的怀旧主义。Prigogine 与 Stengers（1984）提醒我们，秩序从扰动中涌现；Harvey 自己也在《叛逆城市》中追问"城市权利"（right to the city）的可能性——不是回到一个理想化的过去，而是在当下的矛盾结构中寻找另一种空间政治的生长点。注意力经济的扩张并非不可对抗，但对抗它的前提，是承认它的阶级本质，而不是将其伪装成中性的技术进步。
At the same time, I refuse to sink into an impotent nostalgia. Prigogine and Stengers (1984) remind us that order emerges from disturbance; Harvey himself, in Rebel Cities, pursues the possibility of the "right to the city"—not a return to an idealised past but the emergence of an alternative spatial politics from within the contradictions of the present. The expansion of the attention economy is not uncontestable, but contesting it requires acknowledging its class character rather than disguising it as neutral technological progress.
我们不是在哀悼过去。我们是在抵抗一种将过去变为废墟、将废墟变为流量、将流量变为资本的循环——同时假装这一切都是进步。
We are not mourning the past. We are resisting a cycle that transforms the past into ruins, ruins into traffic, and traffic into capital—while insisting all along that this constitutes progress.
圣何塞的废地与天津的五大道，在我看来，是同一个问题的不同地理切片：当资产与价值从实体三维空间向数字层迁移，谁承担了这场迁移的成本？答案是那些没有能力将自己的存在转化为数字资产的人：住在老街区的老人，在谷歌承诺的社区广场上做生意的餐厅主，那些没有社交媒体账号、无法为自己的空间"打卡"的人。他们的损失，不进入任何算法的计算，不出现在任何季报的资产负债表，却是真实的、不可逆的。
The wasteland of San José and the Five Avenues of Tianjin appear to me as different geographical cross-sections of the same problem: when assets and value migrate from physical three-dimensional space to the digital layer, who bears the cost of that migration? The answer is those without the capacity to convert their existence into digital assets: the elderly residents of old neighbourhoods, the restaurant owners who did business in the community squares promised by Google, those without social media accounts who cannot "check in" on behalf of their own space. Their losses do not enter any algorithm's calculations, do not appear on any quarterly balance sheet—yet they are real, and irreversible.

VI.
结语：废墟不会说谎
Concluding Remarks: Ruins Do Not Lie
废墟是诚实的。它不承诺任何东西，不掩饰任何东西。圣何塞的空地，裸露着被拆除的历史建筑的地基；天津五大道的修缮外立面背后，是换了一拨又一拨人的内部空间。两者都在以沉默的方式陈述同一件事：资本曾经来过，然后走了，留下的是别人的损失。
Ruins are honest. They promise nothing and conceal nothing. The cleared lot in San José exposes the foundations of demolished historic buildings; behind the restored facades of Tianjin's Five Avenues lies interior space that has cycled through one occupant after another. Both are stating the same thing in silence: capital was here, and then it left, and what remained was someone else's loss.
文明的珍宝之所以珍贵，在于它们是不可再生的——梁启超在天津写下的文字，Patty's Inn 里几十年的欢笑与争吵，都不能被算法复现。注意力经济恰恰在这一点上显示出其对文明的根本性漠视：它需要内容，而不是历史；它需要流量，而不是记忆；它的时间尺度是季度报告，而文明的时间尺度是世纪。这种尺度上的错位，不是技术问题，而是价值观的对峙。
The treasures of civilisation are precious precisely because they are non-renewable. The texts Liang Qichao wrote in Tianjin, the decades of laughter and argument inside Patty's Inn—none of this can be reproduced by algorithms. It is on this point that the attention economy reveals its fundamental indifference to civilisation: it needs content, not history; it needs traffic, not memory; its temporal scale is the quarterly report, while civilisation's temporal scale is the century. This mismatch of scales is not a technical problem. It is a confrontation of values.
我们身处这场对峙的裂缝之中，既无法忽视数字技术改造世界的现实力量，又无法接受以"进步"之名对历史的系统性清除。我选择站在废墟一侧——不是因为我眷恋废墟，而是因为废墟是诚实的，而那些承诺在废墟上建造乌托邦的声音，往往不是。Harvey（2003）曾引用 Marx 对巴黎公社的评述：被 Haussmann 摧毁的旧城区的人们，在公社运动中爆发——不是因为怀旧，而是因为他们被剥夺了在城市中存在的权利。这个权利，在每一个时代，都值得为之战斗。
We exist in the fissure of this confrontation—unable to ignore the real force with which digital technology is remaking the world, yet unable to accept the systematic erasure of history conducted in the name of "progress." I choose to stand on the side of the ruins—not because I cherish ruins, but because ruins are honest, and the voices that promise to build utopias upon them so often are not. Harvey (2003) invoked Marx's account of the Paris Commune: the people displaced by Haussmann's destruction of the old city erupted in the Commune—not out of nostalgia, but because they had been dispossessed of the right to exist within the city. That right, in every era, is worth fighting for.
