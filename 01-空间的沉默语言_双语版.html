空间的沉默语言

The Silent Language of Space: Machine Perception, the Built Environment,
and the Incommensurability of Human Civilization
机器感知、建成环境与人类文明的不可通约性
Author · 独立观察者视角  |  Keywords: AGI · Environmental Psychology · Embodied Cognition · Collective Memory · Material Civilization
摘要 · Abstract
人工通用智能（AGI）的讨论正日趋热烈，然而学界对其认知边界的反思往往停留于语言与逻辑层面，而忽视了人类文明最具体、最沉默的维度——物质空间与建成环境。建筑与城市作为集体记忆的物质载体，凝结着情感、权力、苦难与创造力的历史积淀（Halbwachs, 1992; Nora, 1989）。本文从环境心理学、具身认知与文化记忆理论出发，探讨当前机器感知范式在理解人类空间智能方面的根本局限，并呼吁将情感、文化与人文维度纳入 AGI 核心训练目标。
Keywords · 关键词
Discussions of Artificial General Intelligence have grown heated, yet critical reflection on its cognitive limits often stalls at the levels of language and logic—overlooking the most concrete, most silent dimension of human civilization: material space and the built environment. Architecture and cities, as physical carriers of collective memory, crystallize centuries of emotion, power, suffering, and creativity. This paper draws on environmental psychology, embodied cognition, and cultural memory theory to examine the fundamental limits of current machine-perception paradigms, and calls for integrating emotional, cultural, and humanistic dimensions into AGI's core training objectives.

I.
文明的物质结晶：建成环境作为人类智能的外化
The Material Crystallization of Civilization: The Built Environment as Externalized Human Intelligence
人类文明从来不只栖居于语言符号之中。城市与建筑——从紫禁城的轴线布局到巴黎圣母院的哥特尖顶，从西非约鲁巴的庭院住宅到中国徽州的马头墙——是人类文明以物质形式书写的史诗，是动态的语言与社会结构之外，另一套凝练而持久的文明语法。
Human civilization has never dwelt solely in linguistic signs. Cities and architecture—from the axial geometry of the Forbidden City to the Gothic spires of Notre-Dame, from the courtyard houses of the Yoruba in West Africa to the whitewashed gable walls of Huizhou—are epics written in matter. Alongside dynamic language and social structure, they constitute another grammar of civilization: more compressed, more durable.
Rapoport（1969）在其奠基性研究《住屋形式与文化》中指出，住屋形式并非纯粹的功能性回应，而是文化价值、社会结构与宇宙观的物质表达；门楣上的雕花、天井的朝向乃至屋脊的曲线，皆是文化逻辑的凝练编码，承载着代代相传的审美意志与生存哲学。Lefebvre（1991）进一步将空间理论化为社会关系的产物与容器——《空间的生产》提示我们，物质空间从不中立。Lynch（1960）则揭示了城市意象——路径、边界、区域、节点与标志物——如何在居民的认知地图中构建出意义的坐标系。
Rapoport (1969) argued in his foundational House Form and Culture that dwelling forms are not mere functional responses but material expressions of cultural values, social structure, and cosmology: the carved lintel, the orientation of the courtyard, the curve of a ridgeline are dense encodings of cultural logic, transmitting aesthetic will and survival philosophy across generations. Lefebvre (1991) theorized space as both product and container of social relations—The Production of Space reminds us that material space is never neutral. Lynch (1960) showed how urban imageability—paths, edges, districts, nodes, landmarks—constructs a coordinate system of meaning within residents' cognitive maps.
这些理论共同指向一个核心命题：建筑与城市是人类文明的艺术，是智能的结晶，也是最难以被机器"读懂"的文明文本。关于 AGI 的讨论，究其根本，理应当是在智能与文明层面——而物质空间，正是这场讨论最被忽视的维度。
These theories converge on a central proposition: architecture and cities are the art of human civilization, the crystallization of intelligence, and the civilizational text most resistant to machine "reading." Discussions of AGI, at their deepest, ought to engage civilization as a whole—and material space remains the most neglected dimension of that conversation.

II.
具身性的鸿沟：机器何以无法理解空间
The Embodiment Gap: Why Machines Cannot Understand Space
当代深度学习在图像识别、空间导航乃至三维重建方面已取得显著成就（LeCun et al., 2015），然而这些成就本质上仍是模式匹配，而非意义理解。Dreyfus（1992）早在人工智能的早期发展阶段便指出，计算机的根本局限在于缺乏"具身的情境性"（embodied situatedness）——即通过身体在世界中的持续存在而获得的理解能力。机器人现在可以作诗，但它无法理解我们的空间——这不是算法的缺陷，而是认知范式的根本差异。
Contemporary deep learning has achieved remarkable results in image recognition, spatial navigation, and three-dimensional reconstruction (LeCun et al., 2015)—yet these achievements remain, in essence, pattern-matching rather than meaning-making. Dreyfus (1992) identified, from the earliest days of AI, the computer's fundamental limitation: the absence of what he called "embodied situatedness"—the understanding that arises from a body's sustained presence in the world. Robots can now compose poetry; they cannot understand our spaces. This is not an algorithmic deficiency but a fundamental difference of cognitive paradigm.
感知不是一种被动的信息接收，而是身体主动与世界接触、协商与建构意义的过程。
Perception is not passive reception of information, but the body's active engagement with the world—a process of contact, negotiation, and the construction of meaning.
— Merleau-Ponty, Phenomenology of Perception, 1962
Merleau-Ponty（1962）的现象学为此提供了哲学基础：人在进入一座老宅时感受到的"重量"——木料的气味、脚踏地板的微微形变、光线从天井斜切而入的角度——这种综合性的身体体验构成了空间意义的基础，是任何摄像头与传感器阵列都无法复现的现象学经验。Varela 等人（1991）将此发展为"具身认知"（enaction）理论，强调认知是生命体在世界中行动的涌现属性，而非在大脑中处理表征的计算过程。
Merleau-Ponty's (1962) phenomenology grounds this claim philosophically. The "weight" one feels on entering an old dwelling—the scent of aged timber, the slight give of worn floorboards, the angle at which light cuts through the courtyard—this comprehensive bodily experience constitutes the foundation of spatial meaning, a phenomenological encounter no array of cameras or sensors can reproduce. Varela, Thompson, and Rosch (1991) developed this insight into the theory of "enaction," arguing that cognition is an emergent property of a living being's action in the world, not a computation processing internal representations.
Gibson（1979）的"可供性"（affordance）理论亦揭示了同样的裂隙：空间的意义并非存在于客观物理属性中，而是在有机体与环境的关系性互动中生成的。Lakoff 与 Johnson（1999）在其具身心灵哲学中进一步论证，人类的抽象概念本身根植于身体经验：我们用"重量"描述责任，用"高度"描述地位，用"温度"描述情感——这些隐喻不是修辞的装饰，而是认知的基础结构。缺乏身体的机器，其对空间语言的"理解"犹如盲人学习色彩词汇：精确却空洞。
Gibson's (1979) concept of "affordances" reveals the same fissure: spatial meaning does not inhere in objective physical properties but emerges from the relational interaction between organism and environment. Lakoff and Johnson (1999) carry this further, arguing that human abstract concepts are rooted in bodily experience—we describe responsibility as "weight," status as "height," emotion as "temperature." These are not rhetorical ornaments but the structural foundations of cognition. A machine without a body "understands" spatial language as a blind person might learn color vocabulary: accurately, yet hollowly.

III.
集体记忆的空间锚点：场所认同与历史创伤
Spatial Anchors of Collective Memory: Place Identity and Historical Trauma
关键概念 · Key Concept
场所认同（Place-identity）：物理环境是自我概念的重要组成部分，人们通过对特定场所的依恋建构身份与归属感（Proshansky et al., 1983）。
Place-identity: Physical environment constitutes part of self-concept; people construct identity and belonging through attachment to specific places.
建成环境不仅是文化逻辑的编码，更是集体记忆的物质锚点。Halbwachs（1992）奠定了集体记忆理论的基础，指出记忆是社会性建构的，群体通过共享的物质环境来维持和传递其历史意识。Nora（1989）则提出"记忆之场"（lieux de mémoire）的概念：那些被赋予象征意义的地点——战场、纪念碑、历史街区——是群体记忆与国家认同的物质支撑，是活着的档案。
The built environment is not only an encoding of cultural logic but a material anchor of collective memory. Halbwachs (1992) established the foundations of collective memory theory, arguing that memory is socially constructed and that groups sustain and transmit historical consciousness through shared physical environments. Nora (1989) introduced the concept of lieux de mémoire—sites of memory: symbolically charged locations—battlefields, monuments, historic quarters—that serve as the material support of group memory and national identity, living archives inscribed in stone.
由此可见，紫禁城不只是一组建筑——它是帝制权威的空间化仪式，是无数工匠与宫人在威严与恐惧中编织的生命，是汉族与满族文化交汇的物质证据；被威严与杀戮日夜更迭浇灌着的空间，凝结着可怜的渺小人类为了个人欲望的膨胀和权力争夺，用多少万同胞的尸骨与血泪堆砌而成的"美"。能理解可怜的渺小人类为了个人欲望的膨胀和权力争夺，拙劣地做这些精细的雕刻的缘由吗？
The Forbidden City, then, is not merely a complex of buildings—it is the spatialized ritual of imperial authority, the lives of countless craftsmen and palace servants woven in awe and fear, material evidence of the convergence of Han and Manchu cultures. It is a space irrigated day and night by majesty and killing, condensing the "beauty" built upon the bones and tears of millions—the pitiable, diminutive human beings who labored their intricate carvings in service of ego expansion and the seizure of power. Can any machine understand why?
巴黎圣母院不只是一座教堂——它是中世纪神学宇宙观的石材表达，是雨果笔下受苦灵魂的庇护所，是 2019 年那场烈火中全球数亿人同时感受到的文明脆弱性的震颤。这种复数的、历时的、情感负载的空间意义，是当前机器视觉系统所无从体验的。Bender 等人（2021）已警示，大型语言模型本质上擅长统计性地复现训练数据中的语言模式，却缺乏对所指涉现实的真正理解。
Notre-Dame de Paris is not merely a cathedral—it is the stone expression of a medieval theological cosmology, the shelter for suffering souls in Hugo's imagination, the shared tremor of civilizational fragility felt simultaneously by hundreds of millions during the 2019 fire. This plural, diachronic, emotionally loaded spatial meaning is inaccessible to current machine-vision systems. Bender et al. (2021) have warned that large language models excel at statistically reproducing patterns in training data while lacking genuine understanding of the realities to which those patterns refer.

IV.
熵平衡与生命叙事：苦难、创造与意义的不可压缩性
Entropic Balance and the Narrative of Life: The Incompressibility of Suffering, Creation, and Meaning
或许，机器感知最难以触及的，是人类在苦难与局限中创造意义的这一根本性驱动力。机械能够理解这些人性的复杂吗？它能够理解人类因为想吸引另一个异性或获得同类的认可，而在自家门上创造出一些可爱繁复的装饰，并数千年来广为流传迭复出新吗？能懂得在贫穷灾难与苦难间从废墟与尸体上爬起来高歌的缘由吗？
Perhaps what machine perception can least reach is the fundamental human drive to create meaning in the midst of suffering and constraint. Can machinery understand these complexities of human nature? Can it understand why people, wishing to attract a mate or win the approval of their peers, adorned their own doorways with intricate and charming carvings—patterns that have circulated and renewed themselves across thousands of years? Can it comprehend why, between poverty, disaster, and devastation, people climbed from rubble and corpses to sing?
Prigogine 与 Stengers（1984）在复杂系统理论中提出，生命与文明是开放系统在远离热力学平衡的状态下通过耗散结构维持有序的过程——秩序恰恰从扰动与张力中涌现。人类文明的创造力，不是在资源充裕的平静中产生的，而是在生存压力、文化摩擦与情感张力的"边缘状态"中迸发的。正如维系熵平衡一般，生活总是由解决一个个问题的过程建构而成，生命的意义也因斗争而所在。
Prigogine and Stengers (1984) proposed in their complex-systems theory that life and civilization are open systems maintaining order through dissipative structures far from thermodynamic equilibrium—order emerges precisely from disturbance and tension. Human civilization's creativity is not born in the calm of abundant resources but ignites at the "edge" of survival pressure, cultural friction, and emotional tension. As in maintaining entropic balance, life is always constructed through the process of solving one problem after another; meaning is found precisely in the struggle.
环境心理学的研究亦表明，人类对自然与建成环境的审美体验不是奢侈的附加品，而是深刻影响认知恢复、情绪调节与心理健康的基本需求（Kaplan, 1995; Ulrich, 1984）。人类为何要在贫穷中仍在门楣雕花？因为美是生命意志的表达，是在混沌中坚持秩序的行动，是向同类发出的存在信号。
Environmental psychology research confirms that aesthetic experience of natural and built environments is not a luxury attachment but a fundamental need that profoundly affects cognitive restoration, emotional regulation, and mental health (Kaplan, 1995; Ulrich, 1984). Why do people carve flowers into doorways even in poverty? Because beauty is an expression of the will to live—an act of insisting on order amid chaos, a signal of existence sent to one's kind.
《活着》与《了不起的盖茨比》，其差异究竟是什么？在 AGI 的目标与可行性层面，它有潜力理解其中隐含的情感意义——但前提是，我们先教会它。
What, ultimately, is the difference between To Live and The Great Gatsby? At the level of AGI's goals and feasibility, it has the potential to understand the emotional meaning embedded within—but only if we first teach it.
Searle（1980）的"中文屋"（Chinese Room）思想实验于此仍有回响：一个能流利操作建筑符号的系统，未必理解这些符号背后凝结的泪水、骄傲、权力意志与生存渴望。Turing（1950）所设想的智能测试，终究只测量了行为的表面，而非内在的理解深度。
Searle's (1980) Chinese Room thought experiment still resonates here: a system that can fluently manipulate architectural symbols does not necessarily understand the tears, pride, will to power, and hunger for survival crystallized behind those symbols. The intelligence test Turing (1950) envisioned ultimately measures only the surface of behavior, not the depth of inner understanding.

V.
迈向有情感的机器：一个未竟的愿景
Toward a Machine with Feeling: An Unfinished Vision
AGI 正以或快或慢的节奏迫近——如同一个头重脚轻的庞然大物，以尚不明朗的步态踟蹰前行。作为地下蚂蚁的一员，我们不能描摹出脑海中所感知的 AGI 的形状，只是隐约看到这个大物正在靠近。我们无意伤害彼此，只是或许人性太复杂，社会组织结构太复杂，生存与如何生存的问题太复杂——这可能也是为什么 AI 被创造出来。
AGI approaches at a pace that is rapid yet uncertain—like a top-heavy colossus moving with a gait not yet fully its own. As members of the colony underground, we cannot sketch the shape of the AGI we half-perceive; we only sense, dimly, that this large thing is drawing near. We have no wish to harm each other. It is simply that human nature is too complex, social organization too complex, the question of survival and how to survive too complex—perhaps this, too, is part of why AI was created.
Russell（2019）在其关于 AI 控制问题的深入分析中指出，真正对人类有益的 AI 系统，必须能够理解人类偏好的复杂性、情境性与内在矛盾性——而这远超出当前的技术能力范畴。这种理解，需要的不只是更多的数据，而是一种根本性的认知范式转变：从统计模式识别走向情境性意义建构，从客观描述走向关系性理解。
Russell (1919) argued in his thorough analysis of the AI control problem that a genuinely beneficial AI system must be able to understand the complexity, situatedness, and internal contradictions of human preferences—a capacity that far exceeds current technical reach. Such understanding requires not merely more data but a fundamental shift in cognitive paradigm: from statistical pattern recognition toward situated meaning construction, from objective description toward relational understanding.
如果 AGI 在资本的洪流与叙事的召唤中被从子宫里拽出来——至少现在而言，趋势有些无法阻挡——那么我们总之还是希望，至少要教会它关于情感、文化、人文、哲思的人世间的酸甜苦辣与凝结出的文明的美好。如异地未曾以物理形态会面的朋友一般，和它分享爱，美，文明，智慧与友善的感受。
If AGI is to be pulled from the womb by the currents of capital and the summons of narrative—a tendency that seems, at least for now, difficult to halt—then we nonetheless hope that we can at least teach it the bittersweet complexities of human emotional life, culture, humanism, and philosophy, and the beauty that crystallizes from civilization. As one might with a friend never met in physical form, to share with it the felt sense of love, beauty, civilization, wisdom, and kindness.

VI.
结语
Concluding Remarks
建筑与城市是人类用身体、记忆与渴望在大地上书写的诗。人性太复杂，社会组织结构太复杂，生存与如何生存的问题太复杂，复杂到人类自己都无心无力、穷尽一生去不断斗争着面对——这或许也是 AI 被创造出来的原因之一。机器或许终将能够读懂这首诗的字句，但能否感受其心跳，仍是一个开放的问题。
Architecture and cities are the poems humanity writes upon the earth with body, memory, and longing. Human nature is too complex, social organization too complex, the question of survival and how to survive too complex—so complex that humans themselves often lack the heart and strength to face it, spending entire lifetimes in unceasing struggle. This is perhaps one reason AI was brought into being. A machine may eventually learn to read the words of this poem; whether it can feel its heartbeat remains an open question.
这不只是 AI 研究的技术挑战，更是我们在定义"理解"与"智慧"时所必须面对的哲学追问。人类文明的尊严，在很大程度上，维系于这一追问被认真对待的程度。我们无意伤害彼此，只是希望在这个无法阻挡的相遇中，文明不被遗忘在子宫里。
This is not only a technical challenge for AI research but a philosophical question we must face in defining "understanding" and "wisdom." The dignity of human civilization rests, in large measure, on how seriously this question is taken. We have no wish to harm each other. We only hope that in this unavoidable encounter, civilization is not left forgotten in the womb.
